Connecting a WhatsApp Business number is the step that takes your bot live. It runs through Meta — the company that owns WhatsApp — so most of this page is about Meta's side of the process: what Meta Business Suite is, how business verification works, and what you'll see as you click through Meta's signup. Paige launches that signup from inside your dashboard and handles the technical wiring (the API token and webhooks) for you, so you never leave Paige and never touch code.

<Note>
  New to the WhatsApp Business Platform? Read [Which WhatsApp product, and when](/learn/whatsapp-products) first. It explains why the Platform — the programmable version Paige builds on — needs a Meta business account and a dedicated number in the first place. This page assumes you've decided the Platform is right for you.
</Note>

## What you'll need

- A **Facebook account**. Meta uses it to identify you and tie your business assets together. It doesn't need a Facebook Page or any posts — it's just the login Meta recognises you by.
- A **phone number** for WhatsApp. It can be a mobile or landline number, but it **must not** already be running as a WhatsApp account — personal or Business App — on a phone. Many businesses use a fresh number to avoid disruption.
- A few **business details** — your legal business name, address, website, and (for verification) a document or two that proves the business is real. More on that below.

<Warning>
  If the number you want is currently a personal WhatsApp account, delete that account in the WhatsApp app before you register it here. That step can't be undone — you'll lose the chat history on it.
</Warning>

## Meta Business Suite, in one minute

The WhatsApp Business Platform isn't a standalone app you sign up for — it lives inside **Meta Business Suite** (you may also see it called Meta Business Manager), the same place businesses manage Facebook Pages, Instagram, and ad accounts. Meta groups everything a business owns — including your **WhatsApp Business Account**, or **WABA** — under one business portfolio there.

Two terms are worth knowing before you start, because they show up throughout signup:

- **Business portfolio** — the top-level container for everything your business owns in Meta. You'll create one during signup if you don't have one.
- **WhatsApp Business Account (WABA)** — the asset inside that portfolio that holds your phone number(s) and messaging settings. One WABA can hold several numbers; each Paige project connects to one number.

You don't need to set any of this up in advance. Meta's embedded signup — the flow Paige launches — creates the portfolio and the WABA for you as you go. Knowing the words just makes the screens easier to follow.

## Verifying your business

Meta wants to know a real business is behind a number before it grants full sending power. That's **business verification**, and it's done in Meta Business Suite, separately from connecting your number.

**You can connect and start messaging before you're verified.** An unverified number works — it just runs under tighter limits (a lower cap on how many new people you can message a day, and a stricter path to a custom display name). Verification lifts those limits. So the usual order is: connect your number now, start testing and going live, and submit for verification in parallel.

<Accordion title="What Meta asks for" icon="file-check">
  Verification confirms your business's legal name, address, and phone number. Meta checks these against official records, so they must match your paperwork exactly. You'll typically be asked for **one document that proves the business is a legal entity** (for example a certificate of incorporation, a business licence, or a tax registration) and sometimes **one that confirms the address** (such as a utility bill or bank statement in the business's name). {/* TODO: verify — Meta's exact accepted-document list changes by country; link out to Meta's current help article rather than enumerating here */}
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="How long it takes" icon="clock">
  Meta usually reviews verification within a few business days, though it can be faster or slower depending on your documents and region. You submit once and wait for Meta's decision — there's nothing to do in Paige while it's in review.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What you can and can't do while waiting" icon="hourglass">
  You **can** connect your number, build, test on [Paige Dev](/quickstart), deploy, and message customers within the daily limit of an unverified number. You **can't** raise those limits or lock in a fully custom display name until verification clears. Nothing you build is wasted — verification only widens what an already-working number can do.
</Accordion>

## Your business info: display name and category

Two pieces of business info shape how you appear to customers, and both have downstream effects worth understanding before you pick them.

**Display name** — the name customers see at the top of the chat. Meta reviews every display name against your verified business and its own [display name guidelines](/whatsapp-setup#display-name-approval), so it should be your real brand name, not a generic word like "Support." A name that doesn't match your verified business is the most common rejection. Until verification and name approval complete, your number can still send — it just shows a less-polished name.

**Category** — the industry that best describes your business (retail, hospitality, professional services, and so on). It sets how your business is classified in Meta's systems and appears on your business profile. Pick the closest honest match; a wildly off category can slow verification.

<Note>
  You set your final display name, profile photo, and business profile details inside Paige after connecting — see [Update your business profile](/whatsapp-setup#update-your-business-profile) below. During signup, just give Meta a sensible business name to start with.
</Note>

## Connect your number

You connect WhatsApp from inside your project. Open your project, go to **Settings**, and select the **WhatsApp** tab in the sidebar — that's `/projects/<your-project-id>?tab=settings&section=whatsapp` if you want to link straight to it. Everything below happens on that one screen. (If your bot isn't connected yet, you'll also see a **Connect** button in the project header that jumps you here.)

## Understanding the connection details

Once connected, the WhatsApp tab shows what Meta reports about your account. Under **WhatsApp Business Account** you'll see:

- **Account Name** — the name on your WhatsApp Business Account.
- **Status** — the account's standing with Meta.
- **Business Verification** — whether Meta has verified your business (see [Verifying your business](/whatsapp-setup#verifying-your-business)).
- **Currency** and **Timezone** — the account's billing currency and time zone, set from your Meta business settings.

The technical identifiers behind the scenes — your WABA ID, phone number ID, and access token — are handled by Paige and kept securely on the server. You don't need them for day-to-day use, and Paige manages token storage and refresh automatically.

## Update your business profile

After connecting, **Settings → WhatsApp** is where you shape what customers see when they open a chat with you. It's laid out in two columns.

### On the left: your number and its health

- **Profile photo** — your logo, via **Upload Photo**. JPEG or PNG.
- **Phone number** — the connected number. Read-only.
- **Display name** — the name customers see. Changing it needs Meta's approval; see below.

Below those, four numbers Meta reports about your number. They're worth glancing at now and then, because they're how you find out you're in trouble before your customers do:

| | What it tells you |
|---|---|
| **Quality Rating** | How customers are reacting to your messages. **Green** is healthy; **yellow** means Meta has noticed blocks or reports; **red** means your sending is at risk. |
| **Throughput** | How fast you can send — Standard is 80 messages a second, High is 1,000. |
| **Messaging Limit** | How many *different* customers you can start a conversation with in 24 hours. Starts at 1,000 and rises as your quality stays good. Relevant when planning a [broadcast](/guides/broadcasts). |
| **Business Verification** | Whether Meta has verified your business. Unverified numbers face tighter limits. |

<Tip>
  A yellow or red quality rating is a signal to slow down and look at what you're sending. Ratings fall when people block or report you — usually after a broadcast to an audience that didn't expect it. Left alone, it costs you your messaging limit and eventually your ability to send.
</Tip>

### On the right: your business profile

Six fields, all under **Business Profile**:

- **About** — a short line on your profile, up to 139 characters.
- **Description** — a fuller description, up to 512 characters.
- **Address**
- **Email**
- **Websites** — up to two.
- **Industry** — pick the closest match from Meta's list.

Click **Save Profile** when you're done. These take effect immediately — only the display name needs review.

## Claim a WhatsApp username

WhatsApp lets a business claim a **username** — an `@handle` customers can find you by without knowing your number. It's in the **Username** field in Settings → WhatsApp.

Type the handle you want and click **Claim**. If you already have one, the button reads **Change** instead, and **Remove** gives it up.

Your username has a status:

- **reserved** — it's yours, waiting on Meta.
- **approved** — live.

<Note>
  Usernames are globally unique across all of WhatsApp, and a number can hold one at a time. Meta decides who gets what — Paige just passes your request along, so a name can be refused for reasons outside Paige's control.
</Note>

If you haven't claimed one, Paige suggests handles based on your brand — click one to claim it directly.

If the handle you want is already on **another of your own numbers**, you'll be told so and offered **Transfer & claim**, which moves it across. It comes off the other number in the process.

## Share your number

The **Share Your WhatsApp** card — under **Settings → Project** — gives customers a way in that isn't typing a phone number:

- A **QR code** they scan to open a chat. **Download PNG** to put it on a poster, a receipt, or a shop window.
- A **wa.me link** to put on your website or in an email signature. **Copy** it.
- A **Pre-filled Message** so the chat opens with text already typed — "Hi, I'd like to book a table" — which gives your bot something to work with from the first message.
- **Share** buttons for WhatsApp, LinkedIn, and Instagram. Instagram has no share API, so that one copies the link for you to paste.

<Note>
  If you haven't connected a number yet, this card shows the **Paige Dev** test number instead, so you can scan and try your bot. That QR is for testing only — don't put it in front of customers.
</Note>

### Display name approval

When you request a display name change, Meta reviews the new name against your business verification status and brand guidelines. This process can take from a few hours to several days.

<Note>
  During review, your existing display name remains active. Users won't see the new name until Meta approves it. You'll receive an update in the Paige dashboard once the review is complete.
</Note>

Common reasons Meta rejects display name requests:

- The name doesn't match the verified business name on your WABA
- The name uses a generic term without a brand identifier (for example, "Support" on its own)
- The name implies an official relationship with Meta or WhatsApp

If your request is rejected, you can update your business verification details in Meta Business Manager and submit a revised name.

## Troubleshooting

**The Meta popup closes without completing** — This usually means a browser extension (such as an ad blocker or popup blocker) interrupted the flow. Disable extensions for the Paige domain and try again.

**My phone number verification code isn't arriving** — Make sure the number can receive SMS or voice calls and isn't already registered as a WhatsApp personal account. If you've requested multiple codes, wait at least 10 minutes before requesting another.

**Signup ended in an error** — If the flow fails, the WhatsApp tab shows an error instead of the connected view. Click **Connect WhatsApp** again to restart Meta's signup from the top.
