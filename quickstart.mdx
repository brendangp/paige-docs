title: "Get started: build your first WhatsApp bot with Paige"
sidebarTitle: "Quick Start"
description: "Sign up, create a project, build your first bot with AI, test it on real WhatsApp via Paige Dev, and only connect your own WhatsApp number when you're ready to go live."
---

This guide walks you through everything you need to go from a new account to a working WhatsApp bot. You'll build and test a fully working bot before ever connecting your own WhatsApp Business number — connecting WhatsApp is the last step, and it's what takes your bot live.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create your account">
    Go to [paigeme.dev](https://paigeme.dev) and sign up. You can create an account with your email address or sign in with Google.

    After signing up with email, Paige will send you a verification link. Open the email and click the link to confirm your address before continuing.

    <Tip>
      Google sign-in skips the email verification step and gets you into the dashboard faster.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Create your first project">
    When you log in for the first time, Paige automatically creates a project called **My First Bot** and takes you straight to it. You don't need to do anything — the project is ready to use.

    A project is your workspace for a single bot. It comes with its own bot code, database, conversation history, and settings. You don't need a WhatsApp number to start building — that comes later. Every new project includes a 14-day free trial with full access to every paid feature — Conversations, Broadcasts, Deploy, and your bot running on a connected WhatsApp number — so you can build and prove your bot end to end before subscribing. If you want to build a second bot for a different use case, you can create additional projects from the dashboard at any time.

    <Info>
      If you already have projects from a previous session, Paige opens the most recently used one instead of creating a new one.
    </Info>

    <Tip>
      A short intro walks you through what Paige does when you first open a project — six slides covering prompting, the preview, testing on Paige Dev, connecting your number, deploying, and getting help. **Skip** dismisses it, and you can replay it later from the checklist.

      That checklist lives in the header and tracks the same journey: **Prompted Paige**, **Tried the preview**, **Tested on Paige Dev**, **Connected WhatsApp**, **Deployed your bot**, and **Explored help & docs**. It ticks each row off as you go, so you can always see what's left.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Build your bot with the AI assistant">
    Your project has three columns: **the chat on the left**, whichever **tab you're on in the middle**, and **the phone preview on the right**. The tabs across the top are Code, Flows, Conversations, Broadcasts, Tools, and Settings — plus Preview, which shows and hides that right-hand phone.

    Start on the **Code** tab and type a description of what you want your bot to do into the chat. For example:

    > "When someone messages me, greet them by name and ask if they want to know our opening hours or our location."

    The AI reads your request and writes (or rewrites) the bot code for you. You can keep refining it by sending follow-up messages — ask it to add conditions, change the wording, or handle edge cases.

    <Tip>
      You don't need to know how to code to use the AI builder. Describe what you want in plain language and iterate from there. If you do know how to code, the middle column shows you exactly what it wrote.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Test your bot with the preview and Paige Dev">
    You don't need a WhatsApp number to test your bot. Paige gives you two test surfaces:

    - **The in-app preview** — the phone on the right. Message your bot there and it responds immediately, no setup at all.
    - **Paige Dev** — a shared WhatsApp number that runs your bot on real WhatsApp, on your actual phone.

    To use Paige Dev, go to **Settings → Project** and find the **Paige Dev — test on your phone** card. Enter your phone number and click **Link phone**. Message the Paige Dev number from your phone and your project's bot answers.

    Everything you do here runs against your preview copy, so test bookings and test data never touch anything real. See [Testing your bot](/guides/testing) for the full walkthrough.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Connect WhatsApp to take it live">
    This is the last step — and the only step that requires WhatsApp at all. Once your bot is ready for real users, link your own WhatsApp Business number to take it live.

    Open your project and go to **Settings → WhatsApp**. Click **Connect WhatsApp** to start the Meta embedded signup flow. You'll log in to your Facebook account, select or create a WhatsApp Business Account (WABA), and choose the phone number you want to use.

    See [Connect your WhatsApp Business number](/whatsapp-setup) for a full walkthrough of this process, including what to do if you don't have a WhatsApp Business Account yet.

    <Note>
      You need a Facebook account to complete the Meta embedded signup. The phone number you connect must not already be registered as a personal WhatsApp account on an active device.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Deploy">
    Connecting a number gives your bot somewhere to live. **Deploy** is what actually puts it there.

    Click **Deploy** in the top-right. Your first deploy switches your bot on — until then it isn't answering real customers, only Paige Dev. From that point on, share your WhatsApp Business number with real users and your bot is live.

    Deploy is also how every later change reaches your customers, and it comes with some choices worth understanding before you press it — see [Deploy](/guides/deploy).
  </Step>
</Steps>
